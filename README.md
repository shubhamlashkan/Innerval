Innerval
A minimalist browser game that tests your internal sense of time. No clock, no countdown, no visual hints — just a target duration and your instincts.
Each round shows you a number — say, 11 seconds. Then it disappears. Hold the circle for exactly that long. Release when you feel the time is up. Your score is how close you were.
How to play

Pick Classic or Daily mode
A target duration flashes on screen — memorise it
Hold the circle until you think the time is up
Release — your accuracy is revealed instantly
Five rounds, then your final score

Game modes
Classic — five randomly generated durations between 2 and 20 seconds, unique every game, no repeats within a session.
Daily — one fixed puzzle per day, same for everyone. Come back tomorrow for a new one.
Scoring
Each round is scored by how close your hold time was to the target. Your final score is the average accuracy across all five rounds. Results include a round-by-round breakdown and an emoji grid you can share on X.
Tech
Pure HTML, CSS, and vanilla JavaScript. No frameworks, no dependencies, no install. Works in any modern browser including mobile Chrome and Safari. Drag interaction uses the Pointer Events API for reliable cross-device support.
